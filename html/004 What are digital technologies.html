The following books

The aim of the books in this section of the ramble - What is digital technology and how might you teach the Digital technologies subject - is to help you answer both these questions. First, to develop your content knowledge so you know what is unique and different about digital technologies. Not only can this knowledge help you develop learning experiences around the Digital technology subject, but it can also help you understand and improve your experiences with digital technologies. This knowledge is then linked to the Digital technologies content descriptions and examples of how people have planned to teach the Digital technologies subject.

Are digital technologies different?

Time to start thinking about digital technologies.

As you've no doubt noticed there is a strong connection within the Technologies learning area. A strong connection between the Design and technologies subject, and the Digital technologies subject. For example, the table on this page from the Technologies learning area showing the similarities between the strands for the two subjects.

For many people this begs the questions:

  • Are digital technologies any different from other technologies?
  • If so, what are the differences?

Brantley-Dias & Ertmer (2013) pose the following

The fundamental question, then, is whether these types of digital tools are sufficiently different from other, more traditional tools, to require their own category of knowledge

Kirschner (2015) is not convinced that digital technologies are any different, nor that they should be treated any different.

Due to the existence of a separate Digital technologies subject, the framers of the Australian Curriculum seem to disagree. There is a difference.

But what is that difference?

The properties and nature of digital technology

The diagram below represents a model of digital technologies. It aims to identify the fundamental properties and the nature of all digital technologies.

It proposes that the fundamental properties for all digital technologies are: data homogenisation and reprogrammability. It is these combination of properties that make a technology "digital".

The model also proposes that these fundamental properties combine to create the distinct nature of digital technologies. The model below suggests that all digital technologies, to varying levels, are: unstable, opaque, and protean.

The proposition is that these properties and nature are what distinguish digital technologies from other types of technologies. Non-digital technologies do not have the same fundamental properties, and are thus do not have the nature of digital technologies.

The remaining books in this ramble will use this model to:

  1. help you develop an understanding of the important concepts associated with digital technologies; and,
  2. connect this understanding with the Digital technologies subject.
