Curriculum, pedagogy and being creative

This week and last week largely focus on introducing you to the learning area, the subjects, and the curriculum. This defines What is to be taught (the content).

In theory, this is different from pedagogy - How it is to be learned/taught.

In reality, separating the two out is not always possible, nor desirable. As mentioned last week Shulman (1987) identified the combination of pedagogy and content knowledge (PCK) as the core knowledge of a teacher.

This week, as with last week, you will see some examples of pedagogy mixed in with the curriculum (content) knowledge, but throughout the rest of the semester you should be looking and thinking about the different types of pedagogies that can be used to teach Digital technologies.
